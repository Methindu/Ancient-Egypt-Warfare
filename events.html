What Happened and how Was it Resolved

The battle of Kadesh was started by a desire to expand egyptian territory, in 1275 BCE, Rameses II was pharaoh and intended to follow expansionist policies. He chose to invade Hittie territory in Palestine, then pushed into Canaan.

At the river of Orontes, the Egyptian army captured two men who clamed to be ex-Hittie, these men were actually hittie spies and gave false information to the egyptians. They said that their army was far from Kadesh taking care of other business, the Egyptians were thrilled at this and decided to launch an attack and get this fight over with.

While the Egyptian were expecting no army to fight with, Muwatallis had an army of 40,000 men and 3,000 chariots, much larger than Egypts 20,000 men and 2,000 chariots. Hittie chariots were also more heavy duty, which made it easy for them to take on the light Egyptian chariots.

However, the Hitties made a terrible military error, rather than finishing the job, they chose to plunder (steal) from Egyptians instead. Rameses II was regrouping his army, ready to counter attack. The Hitties had lost the element of suprise, making them lose a big edge. Also, the Egyptians used their lightweight chariots to outmanouver the Hitties.

The battle ended inconclusively, although Rameses would claim victory of the battle. 15 years later after the death of Muwatallis, both parties signed the first ever peace treaty, today it is known as the treaty of Kadesh.
